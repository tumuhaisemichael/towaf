Who We Are

Solidarity, Partnership, Shared Responsibility

Together With Africa (TOWAF) is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization based in California, USA. Our mission is to restore hope and rebuild lives in post-war and crisis-affected communities across Africa, with a primary focus on the Tigray region of Ethiopia.

The name embodies our belief that sustainable development is achieved through collective action and mutual empowerment, not unilateral aid.

Our Origin

The Catalyst for Change

The Crisis

During the 2021 Tigray war, Dr. Fetien led Mekelle University through siege conditions, coordinating food aid and ensuring student safety amidst immense trauma and destruction.

The Turning Point

A 2022 visit to Tunaydbah Refugee Camp in Sudan revealed the profound psychological trauma of once-prosperous farmers and professionals now reliant on aid. Witnessing this devastation and hearing of refugee suicides solidified her determination to act.

The Action

This led to the informal 'Refugee for Refugee (R4R)' project and a powerful student-mentoring program. Saved by her mother from an arranged marriage at age 12, Dr. Fetien overcame immense challenges to become the first female lecturer at Mekelle University—a trailblazer dedicated to transformative change.

Our
Core
Programs

Our holistic approach covers immediate relief to long-term sustainable development across five key pillars.

Survivors Support

  • Support for women affected by conflict-related sexual violence.
  • Funding for women-owned businesses.
  • 18 prefabricated housing units.
  • Psychosocial resilience training.

Education Recovery

  • S2S-Tigray Peer Mentorship.
  • SAT Center at Mekelle University (400+ students).
  • "University Without Borders" for refugees.
  • Portable STEM labs for youth.

Refugee for Refugee

Supporting PhD students and refugees through education and research collaborations. Two refugee PhD students have already completed their programs.

Tech Infrastructure

Bridging the digital divide by donating 155 computers (Chromebooks, laptops, desktops) to strengthen SAT exam infrastructure and digital access.

Research & Leadership

Graduate research on gender-based violence and reproductive health, plus empowerment programs helping women transform survival into strength.
